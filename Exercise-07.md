# Exercise 07 – Scaling Up Content Creation (Ad Copy Variants)
**Slides:** 13 | **Time:** 3 min | **Tool:** Microsoft Copilot

---

## 🎯 Objective
Take a single piece of ad copy and use AI to generate 10 distinct variants for multivariate testing — demonstrating how AI dramatically accelerates content production at scale.

---

## 📎 Resources Needed
- The Hubbub Facebook ad shown in Slide 13 (or any ad/marketing copy you have)
- Microsoft Copilot

---

## 💬 Solution Prompt

```
Here's a Facebook ad. We'd like to do a multivariate test for copy.

Original ad copy:
"Carving is so last Halloween... want to skip the waste and the mess? 
🎃 #EatYourPumpkin"

I'd like you to come up with 10 different copy alternatives. 

The objective is to get the audience to click through and learn more about 
decorating pumpkins (instead of carving them).

For each alternative:
- Write the ad copy (max 125 characters to fit Facebook's primary text limit)
- Label the persuasion technique being used (e.g., curiosity gap, social proof, 
  urgency, FOMO, humor, contrast, question hook)
- Give a one-sentence explanation of why this approach might work

Organize the 10 alternatives in a table with columns:
| # | Ad Copy | Technique | Why It Works |

Take a deep breath and work step by step.
```

---

## 🔁 Follow-Up Prompt — Pick Your Top 3

```
Based on the 10 alternatives, identify the top 3 you'd recommend for an 
A/B/C test. For each:
- Explain which audience segment it's best suited for
- Suggest what image or visual would pair well with it
- Predict which metric it's most likely to improve (CTR, engagement, shares)
```

---

## 🔁 Adapt to Your Own Context

Replace the pumpkin ad with something relevant to TradeStation. Example:

```
Here's a current ad for our trading platform:
"[INSERT YOUR AD COPY HERE]"

Generate 10 copy variants targeting [retail investors / active traders / 
options traders] who are considering switching from a competitor platform.

Each variant should emphasize [choose one: ease of use / lower fees / 
advanced tools / mobile experience].
```

---

## ✅ What Good Output Looks Like
- 10 genuinely distinct approaches — not just rephrasing the same sentence
- A mix of emotional and rational appeals
- Each variant stays under the character limit
- The technique labels are specific (not just "catchy")
- Follow-up top-3 recommendation has clear reasoning

---

## ⚠️ Watch Out For
- The model may default to similar phrasing across variants — if so, add: *"Make sure each of the 10 uses a completely different persuasion approach."*
- Some variants may be too long for the platform — always check character counts

---

## 🗣 Reflection Questions
1. How long would it have taken you to write 10 quality variants manually?
2. Were there any techniques in the output you hadn't thought to try?
3. How would you evaluate which variant actually wins — what metrics would you use?
