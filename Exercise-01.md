# Exercise 01 – From Dry Press Release to Captivating Blog Post
**Slides:** 16–19 | **Time:** 7 min exercise + 2 min debrief | **Tool:** Microsoft Copilot

---

## 🎯 Objective
Take a formal, corporate press release and transform it into a Monzo-style customer-facing blog post — matching Monzo's transparent, plain-English, friendly brand voice.

---

## 📎 Resources Needed
- **Press Release:** [Experian and Monzo announce new partnership](https://newsroom.experian.co.uk/releases/experian-and-monzo-launch-new-credit-tool.html)
- **Brand Voice Guide:** [Monzo's tone of voice](https://monzo.com/tone-of-voice/)

---

## 💬 Solution Prompt

```
You are a senior content writer for Monzo, a UK-based digital bank known for its 
transparent, jargon-free, and customer-first communication style.

I'm going to give you two things:
1. A press release about a new Experian partnership
2. Monzo's tone of voice guidelines

Your task is to rewrite the press release as a Monzo-style blog post aimed at 
Monzo customers. The blog post should:
- Be written in Monzo's voice: warm, honest, plain-English, no corporate jargon
- Explain what the partnership means for customers in practical, everyday terms
- Be structured with a compelling headline, short punchy paragraphs, and a 
  clear call to action
- Feel like something a real human wrote, not a press team

Step 1: Read the tone of voice guide and summarize Monzo's key writing principles 
in 3 bullet points.
Step 2: Identify the 3 most customer-relevant facts from the press release.
Step 3: Write the blog post (300–400 words) using those principles and facts.

[Paste Press Release text here]
[Paste Tone of Voice key points here]
```

---

## ✅ What Good Output Looks Like
- Short paragraphs (2–3 sentences max)
- No passive voice or corporate phrases like "we are pleased to announce"
- Speaks directly to the customer: "you", "your money", "here's what it means for you"
- Ends with a practical next step or call to action
- Feels like it belongs on Monzo's actual blog

---

## 🔁 Bonus Task
Once the draft is written, ask Copilot:
```
Now generate a photorealistic hero image concept for this blog post. 
Describe what the image should look like in detail.
```

---

## 🗣 Debrief Questions
1. Did the model maintain Monzo's brand voice, or did it slip back into corporate tone?
2. What prompt techniques worked best — did providing the tone guide help?
3. What would you change in the prompt to get a better result?
